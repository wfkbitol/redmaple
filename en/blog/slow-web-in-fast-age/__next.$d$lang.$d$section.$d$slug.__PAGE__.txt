# Slow Web in a Fast Age

I like pages that feel like they breathe when you touch them. They don't throw all their information at you immediately; they give you a moment of air before inviting you forward.

This is also why I built this blog. Many sites chase faster load times, flashier motion, and denser information streams to win attention every second. I want to be more like a tree, giving people a chance to stop and feel the wind.

## Facing information overload

When everything gets faster, the rare thing becomes the pause. A pause means thinking, and thinking means admitting that you are not the center of every problem. The same is true for sites.

I want every post here to have a small buffer. Not to force people to read slower, but to make people more willing to read carefully.

## It's also a demand on myself

Writing is not about leaving behind a "smarter" version of yourself. It is more often about preserving an honest version. For a personal blog, the biggest reward is rarely traffic; it is whether you are willing to present a piece of yourself.
