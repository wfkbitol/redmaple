# Slow Web in a Fast Age

I like pages that feel like they breathe when you touch them. They don't throw all their information at you immediately; they give you a moment of air before inviting you forward.

This is also why I built this blog. Many sites chase faster load times, flashier motion, and denser information streams to win attention every second. I want to be more like a tree, giving people a chance to stop and feel the wind.

## Facing information overload

When everything gets faster, the rare thing becomes the pause. A pause means thinking, and thinking means admitting that you are not the center of every problem. The same is true for sites.

I want every post here to have a small buffer. Not to force people to read slower, but to make people more willing to read carefully.

## It's also a demand on myself

Writing is not about leaving behind a "smarter" version of yourself. It is more often about preserving an honest version. For a personal blog, the biggest reward is rarely traffic; it is whether you are willing to present a piece of yourself.

# The First Breath of the Red Maple

On a long night, the lights of any city can make the world feel still. But once I put my thoughts on the screen, things begin to feel warm, like a maple leaf caught in the wind.

This time, I wanted to make the blog into a "red maple." Not full of cold technical terms, but with a little light in every post—like a small flame hidden behind a falling leaf.

## Why make it this way

I have always felt a personal site shouldn't just be a container for a résumé. It should be more like a public journal, recording the moments when you suddenly understood something or had a new idea.

So I split the site into four parts: blog, gallery, notes, and musings.

- The blog is calmer.
- The gallery is more active.
- Notes are practical and compact.
- Musings hold the occasional fragments.

## What this means

Too often technical work loses its soul by looking too much like a "project." I want this site to keep a bit of personal character: a little warmth, a little edge, and a little romantic mystery.

I hope that when future me opens this post, I can still feel the excitement of building this small world for the first time.
